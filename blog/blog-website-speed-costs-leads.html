Your Slow Website Is Quietly Costing You Leads — Here's the Math

By Sahin Molla · 2026-06-08 · 5 min read

Here is an uncomfortable number: research consistently shows that as page load time goes from 1 second to 5 seconds, the probability of a visitor bouncing increases by roughly 90%. In India, where more than 70% of traffic is mobile — often on crowded networks — speed is not a technical nicety. It is revenue.

The hidden cost in plain numbers

Imagine your ads bring 1,000 visitors a month and 3% convert into enquiries: 30 leads. If a slow site loses even a quarter of visitors before the page renders, you are paying for 1,000 clicks and showing your offer to 750 people. Same ad spend, fewer leads, higher cost per lead. Your competitor with a faster site quietly wins.

The usual suspects we find in audits

Uncompressed images straight from a phone camera, ten plugins doing the job of one, render-blocking scripts, no caching, and hosting chosen purely on price. None of these are exotic problems — they are everywhere, and they are all fixable.

What we do differently

When we build, we build light: modern frameworks like Next.js and React, images served in modern formats, code that ships only what the page needs, and databases (we use Supabase/PostgreSQL) that respond in milliseconds. One brand we rebuilt now serves 186K monthly sessions smoothly — growth that simply could not have stood on its old foundation.

A 5-minute self-check

Open Google PageSpeed Insights, enter your URL, and look at the mobile score. Below 60? You are losing money daily. Between 60–85? There is meaningful room. Above 85? Your foundation is solid — now make the traffic flow.
